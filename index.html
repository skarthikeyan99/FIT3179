Insights on Global Tourism

Global Distribution of International Tourist Arrivals (2018)
This world map visualises the international tourist arrivals for countries in 2018. The colour intensity represents arrival numbers, with darker shades indicating higher tourism levels. North America, Western Europe, and parts of Asia show the highest concentration of arrivals. Africa and parts of South America display lighter shades, suggesting potential for tourism growth. This visualisation helps identify global tourism hotspots and areas with development opportunities.
International Tourism Arrivals by Country and Continent (2018)
This bubble chart illustrates international tourist arrivals by country and continent in 2018. The size and position of each bubble represent arrival numbers. France leads with about 212 million arrivals, followed by the United States and China. Europe dominates with multiple large bubbles, while countries like South Africa and Australia stand out in their respective continents. This visualisation effectively shows both individual country performance and continental trends in global tourism.
Distribution of Arrivals by Region (2018)
This donut chart breaks down international tourist arrivals by region in 2018. Europe dominates with over 50% of global arrivals, followed by Asia & Pacific at about 24%. The Americas account for approximately 15%, while Africa and the Middle East each represent less than 5%. Hover over each segment for exact percentages. This chart highlights the uneven distribution of global tourism and potential growth areas in regions with smaller shares.
Top 10 Countries by Air Passengers Carried (2019)
This bar chart ranks the top 10 countries by air passengers carried in 2019. The United States leads significantly, followed by China and Ireland. Surprisingly, Ireland ranks third, likely due to its role as a hub for European low-cost carriers. India and the United Kingdom complete the top 5. This visualisation provides insights into the scale of different countries' aviation markets and highlights the dominance of large countries and strategic transit hubs in global air travel.
Air Passengers Carried Worldwide (1970 - 2020)
This line chart tracks the global number of air passengers carried from 1970 to 2020. It shows a steady increase over five decades, reflecting the growth of global air travel. The chart reveals rapid growth, particularly from the 1990s onwards, reaching a peak of nearly 4.5 billion passengers in 2019. However, there's a dramatic drop in 2020, plummeting to about 1.8 billion passengers, clearly illustrating the severe impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on international air travel.
